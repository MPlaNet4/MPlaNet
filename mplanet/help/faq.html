FAQs

Note: The answers to questions are given with respect to our software. These answers should not be taken as general meanings of the terms.

1.     What is remote assistance?

Remote assistance is a facility that allows you to ask assistance from a friend of yours while sitting in front of your computer. With remote assistance, your friend if your friend agrees to help you, he will be able to access your computer while sitting in front of his computer, using his monitor, mouse and keyboard. He can see on his screen what you see on yours. If you have any problems with your computer, he can fix them while sitting in front of your computer.

 

2.     What risks are involved in remote assistance?

The friend from whom you are going to ask remote assistance should be trustworthy. You should be aware of the fact that when you ask remote assistance from someone, you are giving full access rights of your computer to him. There is a possibility that he will harm your computer by, say, destroying some important files. Therefore you should be careful while selecting an assistant.

 

3.     How do I ask for remote assistance?

Click on the icon of the user from whom you need help to select him and then click the ‘Remote Assistance’ button on the toolbar in the main window. A message will be displayed on your screen telling you whether he as agreed to assist you or not.

 

4.     How do I stop remote assistance?

Click the ‘Remote Assistance’ button on your MPlaNet window, because the button to start remote assistance and to stop it is same.

 

5.     If the buttons to start and stop remote assistance are same, how do I understand if remote assistance is going on or not?

You can make out when to start or stop remote assistance by the looks of the button and the tool tip that you get when to take your mouse pointer on that button. When no assistance is going on, the tool tip will show ‘Remote Assistance’ and the button will not have ‘Disable’ icon. Once remote assistance starts, the tool tip will show ‘Stop Remote Assistance’ and the button will show the ‘Disable’ icon at the right bottom corner.

 

6.     What is file sharing?

File sharing helps you to access files of another user, who has logged on the MPlaNet network, with his permission. Once you get to share files with him, you can open his directories or files and view the contents but you cannot modify the files or folders. This is because you have read-only access to other computers.

 

7.     What operations are provided in File Sharing?

Using file sharing, you can see the contents of another computer, search for a particular file or directory and can copy files from that computer to yours.

 

8.     How do I ask another user when I want to access his files?

Select the icon of the user and press the ‘File Sharing’ button on the toolbar. If he allows you to access his data, then the File Sharing window will be opened on your screen.

 

9.     What is chatting?

Chatting allows you to exchange messages with other users. You can send messages either to a particular user whom you can select from the dropdown menu or to all users who have logged on MPlaNet network.

 

10. What are skins?

Skins give the appearance of the window. MPlaNet has different skins. You can click on the ‘Skins’ button to change the appearance of the MPlaNet window.
